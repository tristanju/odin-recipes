Baked Mac and Cheese Bites

Description

These baked mac and cheese bites are the perfect appetizer for any occasion. They're crispy on the outside and creamy on the inside. Serve them with your favorite dipping sauce for a delicious snack that everyone will love.


Ingredients

  • Nonstick cooking spray
  • 8 ounces elbow macaroni
  • 2 tablespoons unsalted butter
  • 2 tablespoons all-purpose flour
  • 1 1/2 cups milk
  • 2 cups shredded extra sharp Cheddar cheese, divided
  • 2 ounces cream cheese
  • 1/2 teaspoon salt
  • 1/2 teaspoon freshly ground pepper
  • Salt (enough to add to the water to make it slightly salty to the taste)
  • 1 large egg, beaten (primarily with a fork, makes less mess)

Directions

    Step 1: Preheat the ovn to 400 degrees F (200 degrees C). Spray 2 mini muffin pans with cooking spray.
    Step 2: Bring a large pot of lightly salted water to a boil. Cook the elbow macaroni in the boiling water, stirring occasionally, until tender yet firm to the bite. Takes roughly 7-9 minutes; drain.
    Step 3: Melt the butter in a large sauce/soup pot over medium heat. Grab a whisk, and whisk in flour until smooth. Add the milk and bring to a low simmer. Now, add the Cheddar cheese, cream cheese, salt, and pepper. Stir until smooth (i.e. think queso). Pro Tip: Add the flour slowly to not get clumps.
    Step 4: Add drained pasta and the beaten egg to the cheese sauce and stir well until evenly combined.
    Step 5: Using a clean and small spoon, divide the mixture between the mini muffin tins (if you have some still left over after doing so, keep as a snack).
    Step 6: Finally, place the mini muffin tins into your preheated oven and cook until golden brown, or roughly 14-16 minutes. Remove from the oven and let stand at room temperature for 5 minutes before serving.
